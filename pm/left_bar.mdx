# LargeFileBridge — Left Bar

The **left bar** is the fixed 256px navigation column on every screen. There is **one** left bar —
the **app bar** — data-driven from **`pm/left_bar.yaml`**, which the frontend imports raw
(`src/config/left_bar.ts`, via Vite `?raw`). There is no second code copy of the nav content.

Division of labor:

* **`pm/left_bar.yaml`** — the **machine source of truth** for the *content*: items, labels, icons,
  routes, order, gates, the header label, the account-menu items.
* **This file** — the **human spec** for everything around that content: the wordmark treatment,
  the color rules, states, layout, and acceptance criteria. Where prose here and the yaml disagree
  on content, the yaml wins; on styling, this file wins.

Siblings: [Repos](./repos.mdx) (the landing screen to the right of this bar) ·
[One repo](./one_repo.mdx) (the per-repo file-pinning screen a Repos row opens) ·
[File System](./directories.mdx) (the Finder-style column browser the **File System** item opens) ·
[IPFS](./ipfs.mdx) (the local-pinset view the **IPFS** item opens) ·
[scan.mdx](./scan.mdx) (the scheduleTasks the **Scans** item surfaces) ·
[processing.mdx](./processing.mdx) (the runtime-conditional **Processing** item this bar appends, §3.1).

> **Naming note (pm/menus.mdx, scan.mdx).** The scheduled background jobs are called **"scheduleTasks"**
> internally; the **UI calls what they do "scans."** The former **Sync** nav item is therefore now
> **Scans**. The per-file **Sync** *decision* and the portable **Sync List** keep their names — those
> are different concepts and are unaffected by this rename.

## 1. The wordmark (top of the bar)

The header of the app bar shows the product name as **text only** — no icon mark:

* The label is **"Large File Bridge"** — with a **space between "Large" and "File"** and a **space
  between "File" and "Bridge"**. Never `LargeFileBridge` run together in the wordmark (that spelling
  is only the repo/package name).
* Normal letter tracking (`tracking-normal`) — plain readable text, not a tracked-out logotype.
* It keeps its **brand accent** color (`--lfb-primary`, `#2563eb`) — the accent stays the accent (§2).
* `text-lg font-semibold`, vertically centered in the header band, left-padded to align with the
  nav items, bottom border `--lfb-border`.
* Clicking it routes to `/` (the Repos page).

The label string itself lives in `pm/left_bar.yaml` (`header.label`) so the frontend renders it
verbatim — the yaml carries the spaced form.

Parsing the spaced wordmark (and the rest of the nav content) out of the raw yaml and exposing it to the renderer is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/config/left_bar.ts` — `parse()` function, exported as the `leftBar` config.

## 2. Color rules (LOCKED)

The left bar's text is **black**. The complete rule set:

| What | Was | Now |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nav item labels + icons | medium grey | **black** (`text-black`) |
| Account slot name / email lines | name dark, email medium grey | **both black** (`text-black`) |
| Wordmark "Large File Bridge" | brand accent | **stays accent** (`--lfb-primary`) |
| Active nav item | accent text on accent-tint background | **stays accent** |
| "Sign in" accent in the account slot | accent | **stays accent** |
| Hover background (`hover:bg-slate-100`) | light grey fill | unchanged (it is a fill, not text) |

The principle, verbatim: **everything on the brand accent stays accent; everything that was medium
grey becomes black.** Icons inherit their item's text color, so they follow the same rule
automatically. Black on white passes WCAG AAA; the active state keeps its tint background for shape.

## 3. The app bar (main navigation)

A flat, ordered list under the wordmark; the account slot pinned to the bottom. Content from
`pm/left_bar.yaml` (`Location: app`):

| Order | Item | Icon (lucide) | Route | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Repos** | `FolderGit2` | `/` | Every git repo on this computer (all known automatically — no "managed" opt-in), with its large-file pin counts and status ([Repos](./repos.mdx)). The dedicated long list; the Storages tab links here. |
| 2 | **Storages** | `HardDrive` | `/storages` | The **map of every large-file storage you belong to** ([storages.mdx](./storages.mdx)): a grouping tab whose **children** are **Personal** ([storage_personal.mdx](./storage_personal.mdx)), **one per Company** ([storage_company.mdx](./storage_company.mdx), labeled by company name — derived at runtime), a **Repos** child that routes to the Repos tab (not a long list), and the opted-in **Communities** ([storage_community.mdx](./storage_community.mdx)). | 3 | **File System** | `Columns3` | `/fs` | A Finder-style **column browser** of the whole filesystem, rooted at the home directory, with per-entry **code badges** (R/r repo, P pin, C/c compress, i IPFS) ([File System](./directories.mdx)); also carries **interesting-directory folder coloring** (brown/blue tinting of folders whose subtree holds big files, videos, or heavy images), specified in its hub companion ([file_system.mdx](./file_system.mdx)). |
| 4 | **Devices / Peers** | `Network` | `/devices` | The registry of every **computer** that carries your files — this Mac, a laptop, a studio tower, a server, a family iMac — pinning one another's files over IPFS. **Always shows at least this computer** (auto-seeded, never an empty table); each row is a device identified by its **hardware fingerprint** so similar machines are told apart ([devices.mdx](./devices.mdx)). The label is literally **"Devices / Peers"** — *Devices* is the noun, *Peers* is retained because the devices pin each other. |
| 5 | **Communities** | `Users` | `/communities` | Publishers of large **public** files (videos first) you subscribe to — **Get** their videos to watch, or **Support** them by rebroadcasting to keep them secure. | 6 | **IPFS** | `Boxes` | `/ipfs` | What the local IPFS node is actually pinning — tracked vs. **pinned-but-untracked** content (import already-pinned files) and the node's only-our-content security posture ([IPFS](./ipfs.mdx)). |
| 7 | **Scans** | `RefreshCw` | `/scans` | The scheduled background **scan tasks** ("scheduleTasks"): each `launchd` job's installed / on-off state, the every-15-min and every-4h schedules, and recent activity ([scan.mdx](./scan.mdx)). |
| 8 | **To Do** | `ListTodo` | `/todo` | The one aggregated list of everything LFBridge recommends doing, bundled **per storage** as [TO DO Batches](./to_do_batches.mdx): pull-downs from your other computers, big files to git-ignore, videos/images to compress, files to back up, and media to transcribe. Each batch **with work** renders as a **slug**; only storages with work show ([To Do](./to_do.mdx)). Carries a **count badge** of non-dismissed batches with work. |
| 9 | **Settings** | `Settings` | `/settings` | Account, allow-list, and IPFS node configuration. |

The **Storages** item is a grouping tab with **children** (`children:` in the yaml): Personal · one per
Company (derived from each company storage's `storage.yaml` `company_name`) · Repos (→ the Repos tab) ·
Communities (the opted-in ones). The children render as an indented/disclosure group under the item;
the dynamic company children are inserted at runtime from the discovered storages ([storages.mdx §2](./storages.mdx#2-the-storages-left-bar-tab)).

States:

* **Active** — the item whose route matches the current path (`/` exact; others by prefix):
  accent text + `--lfb-primary`/10 tint background, `font-medium`.
* **Inactive** — black text (§2), transparent background.
* **Hover** — `bg-slate-100` fill; text color unchanged.
* Each item shows its yaml `description` as the hover `title` tooltip.

Rendering each nav item from the yaml with the active (route-match) / hover states and the description tooltip is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar` component (its `isActive` route match).

### 3.1 The runtime-conditional **Processing** item (only while work runs)

A **Processing** item is appended as the **last item of the top nav list** — **after** the static yaml
items (…Scans, Settings) and **before** the empty space that runs down to the pinned account slot (§4) —
**only while background work is running** (a running job, a pending backlog, or an active "Compress inside"
batch), and it **disappears** the moment work finishes. It routes to **`/processing`**. Full behavior — the
three background-work surfaces and the Processing page — is owned by [processing.mdx](./processing.mdx).

Like the dynamic company children under **Storages** (§3), this item is **runtime-injected** — it depends
on live state, so it is **not** a static row in `pm/left_bar.yaml` (it is only *documented* there as a
commented, runtime-only entry). The Sidebar appends it after the yaml `navItems` map when work is running,
in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar`
(reads `useProgress()`; renders the Processing `<Link>` after the yaml items when `processing` is true).

### 3.2 Repos nest under their owner in the Storages disclosure (LOCKED)

The **Storages** disclosure (§3) already lists **Personal**, one child **per Company**, a flat **Repos** link, and
**Communities**. On top of that, **each repo groups under its owner** — the company or Personal it maps to
([repo_company_mapping.mdx](./repo_company_mapping.mdx)) — so the disclosure doubles as an owner-grouped repo tree,
**in addition to** (never replacing) the flat **Repos** child that routes to the long list ([repos.mdx](./repos.mdx)).

* **Where the nesting appears.** The **Personal** child and **each Company** child are **themselves expandable**:
  expanding one reveals a sub-list of the repos owned by it (label = repo name, route = `/repos/$repoId`). A
  company with no repos yet expands to an empty/"No repos" leaf; Personal always has at least the repos with no
  forge org ([repo_company_mapping.mdx §3](./repo_company_mapping.mdx#3-the-heuristic--which-parse-becomes-a-company-locked)).
* **The flat Repos child stays.** Clicking the **Repos** child still routes to the dedicated Repos tab (the whole
  list) — the owner nesting is a *convenience grouping*, not a replacement. A repo therefore appears **twice**: once
  under its owner group, and in the flat Repos list.
* **Runtime-injected, like the company children.** These per-owner repo sub-lists depend on **live** state (the
  discovered repos and their derived/overridden owners), so — exactly like the dynamic per-Company children (§3)
  and the Processing item (§3.1) — they are **not** static rows in `pm/left_bar.yaml`. The yaml documents them as a
  commented, runtime-only shape; `Sidebar.tsx` injects them under each owner child at render time.
* **Auto vs. manual owner.** A company group derived automatically from a forge org
  ([repo_company_mapping.mdx §3.1](./repo_company_mapping.mdx#31-the-honest-limitation-locked)) carries a subtle
  **(auto-detected)** hint on its group header; a user-assigned owner shows no hint. The friendly **display name**
  ([storage_company.mdx §6](./storage_company.mdx#6-the-friendly-display-name)) — not the cryptic org slug — labels
  each company group.

Injecting each owner child's repo sub-list under the Storages disclosure at runtime (grouping the discovered repos by their `owner`, labeling companies by display name) is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar` (builds `storageChildren`, then nests the repos grouped by `RepoRow.owner` under the matching Personal / Company child), reading owners composed by `~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/backend/src/modules/store-model/units.service.ts` — `computeRepoRow()`.

### 3.3 The Storages entry carries a pending-mapping-review badge (LOCKED)

When a teammate has asserted that one or more of **this computer's** repos belong to a company — an ownership
assertion pulled in via the company sync repo's `owner_map.yaml` — the **Storages** nav item shows a small
**count badge** (same badge treatment as the **To Do** item's, §3 row 8) for the number of **pending
company-mapping reviews**. Clicking it (or the Storages item itself) routes to
**`/company-mappings/review`**, the review-and-consent page where the member accepts or declines each
asserted repo before any of their repos are re-mapped. The badge is a **reopen affordance**: a member who
navigated away from the review can always get back to it here. Nothing is applied silently — the badge is the
only nag. The pending set, the assertion travel, and the review page are owned by
[repo_owner_propagation.mdx §4](./repo_owner_propagation.mdx); this bar only surfaces the badge + link.

The badge count is **runtime-injected** (it depends on live pulled state), like the dynamic company children
(§3) and the Processing item (§3.1), so it is not a static row in `pm/left_bar.yaml`. Rendering the
pending-review badge on the Storages item and linking it to `/company-mappings/review` is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar`
(reads the pending-mappings count from `GET /api/company-mappings/pending`, renders the badge on the Storages
`<Link>`).

## 4. The account slot (bottom of the bar)

Pinned to the bottom of the bar, always visible:

* Every session belongs to an **allow-listed, signed-in** user (OpenAuthFederated Google SSO —
  there is **no default / anonymous account**, per the charter). So the slot always renders a
  **signed-in** state: avatar + **name** + **Google email**, both text lines **black** (§2).
* Clicking it opens an **upward-expanding** popup menu (items from the yaml `footer`): **Settings**,
  **Allow-list** (only with `role:admin`), **Log out**. Log out returns to the sign-in screen.

The bottom account slot and its upward-expanding, permission-gated popup menu are handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar` component (the account-menu `permissionGate` filter).

### 4.1 The non-intrusive hover-info panel sits just above this slot (LOCKED)

Directly **above** the account slot — between the scrolling nav list and the slot — the bar reserves a
**fixed block of always-white space** for the **non-intrusive hover-info panel**
([non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx](./non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx)). This is the out-of-the-way home for the
explanations that used to float over content: hover a **code badge**, an **interesting-folder glyph**, or a
**file/directory row**, and its meaning appears **here** instead of in a bubble that covers the row.

* **Reserved height ≈ two nav-item rows.** The bar keeps that much space beneath the last nav item, and it
  stays **blank white at rest** — so the account slot **never jumps** when a hover starts or ends. (This is
  why the nav list is not a bare `flex-1` that eats all the space down to the slot; a fixed panel block is
  carved out above the slot.)
* **Only what fits, never scrolls or grows.** The panel shows a stack of info blocks — a color-key block per
  badge/color and an up-to-three-line detail block for a file/directory — and renders **only what fits** in
  the reserved height; overflow is dropped, and the panel never pushes or overlaps the account slot.
* The panel's content model, sections, and priority order are owned by
  [non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx](./non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx); this bar only **reserves the space and mounts the
  panel** in the right place.

Reserving the fixed white block and mounting the panel between the nav list and the account slot are handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar` (a fixed-height `<div>` inserted between the closing `</nav>` and the account-slot `div`), rendering `~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/hoverinfo/HoverInfoPanel.tsx` — `HoverInfoPanel`.

### 4.2 External product links — the outbound links above the account slot (LOCKED)

In the space **between the bottom of the top nav list and the account slot** — the same in-between region
as the hover-info panel (§4.1) — the bar shows a small set of **external product links**. These are the
**only** entries in the bar that leave the app: each is an **outbound link to one of our other products**
that **opens in a new browser tab**, not an in-app route.

* **The links (content source of truth = `pm/left_bar.yaml` `external_links`):**
  * **JFK Social.com** → `https://JFKSocial.com/`
  * **ACT 3 Filmmaking** → `https://ACT3ai.com/`
* **New tab, safely.** Each renders as a real `<a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">` — clicking
  opens the URL in a **new tab** and never navigates the app itself away.
* **Placement & spacing.** The block sits **centered/evenly spaced** in the gap between the last nav item
  and the account slot: the links are laid out with even vertical spacing (and horizontally centered),
  visually balanced between the top list and the signed-in-user slot at the bottom. It is a normal-flow
  block above the account slot — it must **not** overlap the account slot and it shares the region with the
  hover-info panel (§4.1) without crowding it.
* **Styling.** Black text like the rest of the bar (§2), an outbound/external hint is acceptable (a small
  `↗` glyph), light-grey hover fill (`hover:bg-slate-100`) matching the nav items. They are **not** part of
  the active-route machinery (they never highlight as "active" — they are external).
* **Data-driven, add/remove in the yaml.** Adding or removing a product link is a `pm/left_bar.yaml` edit
  (`external_links`), never a code change — the same "no code copy of the nav content" rule as the nav items
  (§AC4). An empty/absent `external_links` list renders nothing (no empty block).

Rendering the external-links block from the yaml (new-tab anchors, centered/evenly-spaced, between the nav list and the account slot) is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/Sidebar.tsx` — `Sidebar` (an `external_links` block rendered above the account slot), fed by `~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/config/left_bar.ts` — `parse()` (which reads `external_links` into `leftBar.externalLinks`).

## 5. Layout & behavior

* Fixed width **256px** (`w-64`), full height, white background, right border `--lfb-border`;
  the nav list scrolls independently if it overflows.
* Permission gates come from OpenAuthFederated claims (e.g. `role:admin`); a gated item is
  **hidden**, not disabled, when the claim is absent.
* Icons resolve by lucide name in `NavIcon.tsx`; an unknown icon name renders no icon rather than
  crashing.

Resolving a nav item's lucide icon by name (unknown name renders nothing) is handled in code:
`~/BGit/Bryan_git/LargeFileBridge/code/packages/frontend/src/components/layout/NavIcon.tsx` — `NavIcon` component.

## 6. Acceptance criteria

1. The header reads **"Large File Bridge"** — spaces between *Large*/*File* and *File*/*Bridge*,
   normal tracking, brand accent (`--lfb-primary`), and routes to `/`.
2. All left-bar text is **black**: nav labels, icons, and both account-slot text lines. Nothing in
   the bar renders in medium grey.
3. Accent elements keep their accent: the wordmark, the active nav item (text + tint background),
   and the Sign-in accent.
4. The bar renders its content **from `pm/left_bar.yaml`** — items, order, icons, routes, labels,
   and gates; no hard-coded copies in components.
5. The app bar lists exactly: Repos, Storages, File System, **Devices / Peers**, Communities, IPFS,
   Scans, **To Do**, Settings — in that order. (The **To Do** item — [to_do.mdx](./to_do.mdx) — opens the
   aggregated per-storage batch list and carries a count badge of non-dismissed batches with work. The
   **Devices / Peers** item is the renamed former **Peers** item
   — its label reads literally *"Devices / Peers"* and it opens the device registry in
   [devices.mdx](./devices.mdx). The **Scans** item is the renamed former **Sync** item — the
   scheduleTasks surface; §naming note. **Communities** — [communities.mdx](./communities.mdx) — is the
   opt-in page for carrying other publishers' public files.)
6. Active state matches by route (exact `/`, prefix otherwise); gated items are hidden without the
   claim; hover shows the light-grey fill without changing text color.
7. The account slot sits at the bottom, is always signed-in (no anonymous state), and its popup
   expands upward.
8. **Hover-info panel (§4.1):** a fixed block of **always-white space (~two nav-item rows tall)** sits
   **directly above the account slot**, hosting the **non-intrusive hover-info panel**
   ([non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx](./non_intrusive_tooltip.mdx)); it is blank white at rest, shows badge / folder
   / row explanations on hover, shows **only what fits**, and **never** pushes or overlaps the account slot.
9. **Pending-mapping badge (§3.3):** when a teammate has asserted that one or more of this computer's repos
   belong to a company, the **Storages** item shows a runtime **count badge** of pending company-mapping
   reviews and links to **`/company-mappings/review`** ([repo_owner_propagation.mdx §4](./repo_owner_propagation.mdx));
   it is a reopen affordance and nothing is applied silently.
10. **External product links (§4.2):** a block of outbound links — **JFK Social.com** (`https://JFKSocial.com/`)
    and **ACT 3 Filmmaking** (`https://ACT3ai.com/`) — renders **between the bottom of the nav list and the
    account slot**, evenly spaced/centered; each is an `<a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">` that
    opens in a **new tab** (never navigating the app away); the list comes from `pm/left_bar.yaml`
    `external_links` (add/remove without a code change), and an empty list renders nothing.
